What is AI?
Answer: Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence exhibited by machines, particularly computer systems, that can learn and take actions to achieve specific goals (paragraph 1).
What are some applications of AI?

Answer: AI is used in various fields including web search engines, recommendation systems, virtual assistants, autonomous vehicles, creative tools, and game playing (paragraph 2).
When did AI research begin?

Answer: Alan Turing is considered a pioneer in AI research, which began as an academic discipline in 1956 (paragraph 3).
What is the history of AI funding and interest?

Answer: AI research has cycled through periods of optimism and disappointment, with funding fluctuations. Deep learning advancements in 2012 and the transformer architecture in 2017 led to a recent AI boom (paragraph 3).
What are the societal and economic impacts of AI?

Answer: AI is influencing automation, data-driven decision making, and integration into various sectors. This impacts job markets, healthcare, government, and education (paragraph 4).
What are the concerns surrounding AI?

Answer: The long-term effects, ethical implications, and risks of AI raise discussions about regulations to ensure safety and benefits (paragraph 4).
What are the goals of AI research?

Answer: Traditional goals include reasoning, knowledge representation, planning, learning, natural language processing, perception, and robotics support. The ultimate goal is to achieve general intelligence, where machines can perform any human task (paragraph 5).
What techniques are used in AI research?

Answer: AI research incorporates search, mathematical optimization, logic, artificial neural networks, statistics, operations research, and economics. It also draws from psychology, linguistics, philosophy, and neuroscience (paragraph 5).
